The Power of Semantic HTML5

Using semantic tags like `header`, `nav`, `main`, `article`, and `footer` makes your code more readable for humans and more understandable for search engine crawlers and assistive technologies. It's a foundational practice for building robust and accessible websites.

This is a follow-up paragraph explaining more about why semantic tags are important for SEO and accessibility. They provide context and structure that non-semantic tags like `div` simply can't.

Why Accessibility Matters

Creating an accessible website isn't just a legal requirement; it's a moral one. By using attributes like `aria-label`, ensuring proper heading hierarchy, and providing alternative text for images, we ensure our content is available to everyone, regardless of ability.

An accessible site often has a better user experience for all users, and many accessibility best practices also overlap with good SEO practices.
